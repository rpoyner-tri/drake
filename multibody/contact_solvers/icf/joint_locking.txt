===============================================
 Joint Locking Design and Implementation Notes
===============================================

General idea: solve a reduced problem, where selected joint velocities are
artificially fixed to 0.  Numerous consequences.

Observation: client-side item locking adds both velocity locking and collision
filters. So, just punting the implementation of velocity locking would lead to
free bodies falling through tables, floors, etc.

SAP already implements joint locking, constraints, etc., so it should be a
source of design inspiration, even if the code and data are much differently
organized.

* Design idea: supply a reduced icf_model (and data!!) to the icf_solver; leave
  the solver pretty much alone. This structure is at least somewhat parallel to
  the SAP solution of joint locking.

* Question: model and data comprise a ton of stuff, both fundamental (params)
  and derived (internal data). Is it better to offer a facade-style model that
  relies on the full model, or is it better to just build out a reduced model?

* Answer: facade it too complicated for the first implementation. Better to
  just make an entire reduced model/data, and tweak performance later.

* Question: what effect does joint locking have on constraints? Maybe this can
  be answered by looking carefully at SAP.

  * SAP answers this by constructing a clique map, and then eliminating
    constraints where all involved cliques have been removed. For CENIC, we
    have more constraint-type-specific information. 

* Question: it is pretty clear how to map velocities to/from the reduced
  problem; how do bodies map?

  * Answer: leave bodies as-is. Track permutations of velocities and cliques.

* Question: it seems that cliques are somewhat arbitrary; can we exploit them
  to make a reduced mapping more easily (sparsity hacking)?

  * Answer: Too hard for a first implementation. Go big and fuss later.

* Question: how do we handle locking that removes all of the velocities?

  * Answer: write a test, and make sure SAP and CENIC do the same thing.
